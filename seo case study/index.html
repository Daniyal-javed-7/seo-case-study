The Challenge

A trusted contractor.
Zero digital presence.

Apex Ridge Roofing had built a strong local reputation over 11 years entirely on referrals and word-of-mouth. They were skilled. They were trusted. But online, they were invisible — while newer, less experienced competitors were capturing every high-intent Google search in their territory.

They came to me with a clear brief: fix our online presence and turn Google into a reliable lead source. What followed was a complete SEO transformation — from a broken website nobody could find to a dominant local authority in six months.

02 — Initial Situation

Where Apex Ridge
Stood Before

Before any work began, I conducted a complete baseline audit across every dimension of their digital presence. What I found confirmed the opportunity: a business with 11 years of trust and craft — and a website that reflected none of it.

⚠
Before — Baseline Issues
  • Ranking pages 5–9 for primary queries: "roofing company [city]", "roof replacement [city]", "emergency roof repair"
  • Website loading in 8.4 seconds on mobile — well above the 3-second bounce threshold
  • Google Business Profile 40% complete — no photos, services, or description
  • Only 11 Google reviews with no response to any of them
  • Zero location-specific landing pages — a single homepage serving an entire metro
  • 38 broken links and duplicate title tags across 80% of pages
  • No schema markup of any kind deployed
  • Domain Rating of 3 — effectively no backlink authority
  • ~190 monthly organic sessions, almost entirely branded searches
  • 4–6 inbound leads per month from organic. $3,200/month in Google Ads just to compensate.
✓
After — Post-Campaign Results
  • Ranking #1–3 for 18 primary and long-tail roofing keywords across target city
  • Mobile load time reduced to 1.6 seconds — 81% improvement, all Core Web Vitals green
  • Google Business Profile at 100% completion — 200+ photos, full service menu, active posts
  • 68 Google reviews at 4.8★ — fully responded to, with an active review generation system
  • 14 geo-targeted service area pages live and indexed, capturing neighborhood-level intent
  • Zero broken links, all metadata rewritten, clean technical foundation
  • LocalBusiness, Service, Review, and FAQ schema fully implemented
  • Domain Rating grown to 22 — 34 quality referring domains earned
  • ~737 monthly organic sessions — high-intent, non-branded, converting traffic
  • 20–24 inbound leads/month from organic. Google Ads spend reduced by 70%.
03 — SEO Strategy

The Four-Layer
Approach

Every campaign I run follows a deliberate sequence. Foundations before content. Signals before authority. The order matters — and so does the depth of execution at each layer.

01
Technical SEO

Build the Foundation

  • Full crawl audit via Screaming Frog — 38 broken links resolved
  • Core Web Vitals remediation: LCP, CLS, FID all brought to passing
  • Image optimization — WebP conversion, lazy loading, 60% size reduction
  • Mobile-first responsiveness audit and rebuild
  • Canonicalization, 301 redirects, and duplicate content cleanup
  • XML sitemap submission to GSC + robots.txt correction
  • HTTPS enforcement and security header configuration
  • LocalBusiness, Service, FAQ & Review schema markup deployed
02
Local SEO

Own the Map

  • Google Business Profile rebuilt from 40% to 100% completion
  • 200+ professional project photos uploaded with geo-tagged filenames
  • NAP audit and correction across 45+ citation sources
  • Review generation system: post-job SMS + email sequence deployed
  • GBP posting cadence: 4× per week (offers, project showcases, tips)
  • Q&A seeding with 20+ high-intent questions and answers
  • Local link building: 34 referring domains from local news, HOAs, directories
  • 14 neighborhood-level service area landing pages built
03
On-Page Optimization

Earn the Click

  • Keyword research: 180+ opportunities mapped across 4 content silos
  • All title tags and meta descriptions rewritten — CTR-optimized
  • H1–H3 header hierarchy restructured across every service page
  • Internal linking architecture rebuilt to pass authority to deep pages
  • Above-the-fold CTA redesign — phone number, form, trust signals
  • Conversion copy rewrite: benefit-led, local-specific, trust-forward
  • FAQ sections added to all service pages — FAQ schema applied
  • E-E-A-T signals strengthened: certifications, team bios, project gallery
04
Content Strategy

Build Authority

  • 10 long-form blog posts targeting informational + local keywords
  • Content silos: Storm Damage, Roof Replacement, Repairs, Insurance Claims
  • Competitor content gap analysis — 22 untapped keyword opportunities identified
  • 14 localized service pages — one per major neighborhood/suburb
  • Featured snippet optimization for "how much does a roof cost in [city]"
  • Internal content hub structure — all content linking back to money pages
  • Seasonal content calendar aligned to roofing demand peaks (spring/storm season)
  • 3 case study pages showcasing specific completed projects with photos

10 — Strategic Summary

What This Case Study
Demonstrates

The roofing industry is one of the most competitive local service verticals in search — high ticket values, seasonal demand spikes, and a fragmented landscape of contractors with wildly inconsistent SEO maturity. That inconsistency is the opportunity.

Most local roofing contractors have a foundational problem: a website that was built to look decent and then left untouched. No technical maintenance. No local SEO strategy. No content that earns rankings. The gap between a neglected web presence and a well-executed SEO campaign in this space is enormous.

Apex Ridge's transformation shows what happens when all four layers — technical, local, on-page, and content — are executed together with discipline. No single tactic moved the needle. The compounding effect of all four did.

  • 01Technical SEO is the unglamorous work that unlocks everything else. A fast, crawlable, schema-rich website is non-negotiable in 2024.
  • 02The Google Business Profile is the most underutilized asset in local SEO. Most contractors treat it as a listing. It should be treated as a second website.
  • 03Reviews compound. 57 new reviews over 6 months didn't just improve trust — they became the primary driver of Map Pack positioning.
  • 04Geo-targeted pages open search channels that a single homepage can never capture. 14 neighborhood pages = 14 new doors into the website.
  • 05The businesses that win in SEO are the ones who commit past the 90-day patience threshold. Most competitors quit before the compounding begins.
